# The TeleTracking Course Builder — Leadership Highlight Reel

*Prepared by James Epperson · Learning Management Office · June 2026*

---

## The bottom line

The **Course Builder** is an in-house system I designed and built that turns plain
content — a Word doc, a Markdown draft, or an existing course — into a fully
**TeleTracking-branded, interactive course that tracks completion in Intellum**, and
now into **polished, on-brand PowerPoint decks** from the very same source. No
per-seat authoring licenses, no vendor turnaround, no rebuilding for each brand
refresh or language. It runs on an ordinary laptop and rides our existing Claude
subscription — there is nothing new to buy or host.

It is already producing real, shipping work. The question on the table is simple:
**keep investing in it.**

## What it does *now* (shipping today)

- **Two builders, one engine.** From a single content source it produces both an
  **interactive course** (SCORM 1.2 / SCORM 2004 / cmi5-xAPI, tracked in Intellum)
  and an **editable PowerPoint deck**.
- **Brand-faithful by design.** Rebuilt this year to match TeleTracking's **official
  2026 PowerPoint template** — real backgrounds, the design language behind the
  template's own layouts, Open Sans, and correct logo treatment. A swappable brand
  profile means it can be re-skinned for a client without touching the engine.
- **A real slide design language — 16 layouts.** Title/section, infographic, process,
  cards, comparison, timeline, cycles, charts, big-number statements, quotes, and
  image layouts — all adapting to the content rather than forcing content into a box.
- **A slide studio.** Generate a deck, then shape it slide by slide with no code:
  faithful in-browser preview, live thumbnails, per-slide regenerate (reword,
  re-layout, or recolor just one slide), a one-flag **dark/light theme flip**,
  on-brand stock imagery, native charts, and verified entrance animations.
- **Sound learning, built in.** Course drafts apply established adult-learning design
  (Knowles, Merrill, Gagné) automatically and carry a **design rationale** that
  explains *why* each choice was made — reviewable by SMEs.
- **People stay in control.** The system automates the *assembly*; humans own the
  content, the art, the brand sign-off, and the final approval. Nothing publishes
  without a person saying yes.
- **Accessible and secure by default** — screen-reader support, keyboard navigation,
  and a hardened local tool.

## What it will do *soon* (next on the path)

- **More slide layouts** — process variants and an org-chart layout to round out the
  design language.
- **A tighter authoring loop** — preview parity and richer in-dashboard controls so
  more of the work happens before PowerPoint ever opens.
- **More formats from one source** — release notes, one-page job aids, and email
  digests generated from the same content that builds the course.
- **Animation wired through every layout** so generated decks move like hand-built ones.

These are near-term, incremental, and already scoped — not speculative.

## What it could *expand* to (the bigger vision)

- **Product simulations** — guided, click-through practice in real Nova workflows
  (transfers, bed requests), so learners practice in a safe replica before the live system.
- **AI roleplay** — conversational practice for escalation and patient-flow decisions.
- **Customer academies** — scale the same branded enablement out to the hospitals we
  serve, turning training into a customer-success and retention asset.
- **A feedback loop** — use completion and quiz data to spot weak spots and auto-flag
  courses for refresh, so the catalog improves itself over time.
- **A productized internal tool** — the engine is backend-agnostic and needs no AI at
  runtime; if the business wanted, it could be handed to the development team as a
  supported internal app.
