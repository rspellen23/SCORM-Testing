**Slide 2 — Your Role and What Success Looks Like**
You're joining Vantage as an Account Executive. Your job is to find the right companies, earn their trust, and show them — clearly and honestly — how Vantage Flow solves a problem they're already losing time and money to.

In your first 30 days, success looks like this: you've completed this onboarding path, shadowed at least three live discovery calls, built your first target account list in Salesforce, and run one discovery call solo with your manager listening in.

You won't be expected to close deals in your first month. You will be expected to understand the product, know the process, and start building your pipeline. Everything else follows from that.

**Slide 3 — What We Sell**
*Visual:* graphic · Vantage Flow product overview · slot: `vantage_3_product.png`
Vantage Flow is a workflow automation platform that helps operations teams at mid-size companies eliminate manual handoffs, reduce project delays, and get visibility across every department — in one place.

Most of our customers come to us after spending years duct-taping together spreadsheets, email chains, and point solutions that don't talk to each other. Vantage Flow replaces that patchwork with a single connected system that actually reflects how work moves through their organization.

Key benefits:
- Real-time workflow visibility across every team and department
- Native integrations with 150+ business tools — ERP, CRM, HRIS — no custom coding required
- Automated escalations that surface bottlenecks before they become missed deadlines

**Slide 4 — Who We Sell To**

Our ideal customers are companies with 200–2,000 employees in manufacturing, healthcare operations, or financial services — where work is cross-functional, timelines matter, and the cost of a dropped handoff is real.

**Slide 5 — Our Sales Process**
We use a MEDDIC-aligned approach — every deal should have a clear economic buyer, a confirmed business case, and a decision process mapped before we move to proposal.

1. Prospect — Identify accounts that fit the ICP and find a contact at the VP level or above.
2. Connect — Book a 20-minute discovery call. Lead with the pain, not the pitch.
3. Discover — Confirm Metrics (what does the cost of the problem look like?), Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, and Champion.
4. Demo — Show Vantage Flow mapped to their specific pain. Not a generic product tour — their workflow, their language.
5. Propose — Send a tailored proposal within 48 hours of the demo. Include the ROI case your champion can use to sell internally.
6. Close — Work the champion to navigate procurement, legal, and security review. Never lose on process.

**Slide 7 — Your First 30 Days**
Your ramp goal is simple: understand the product, learn the process, and make your first qualified discovery call — all within 30 days. Here's how that breaks down.

- **Week 1:** Complete this onboarding path · Set up Salesforce, Slack, Highspot, and Gong · Listen to five recorded discovery calls in Gong — take notes on the questions top reps ask
- **Week 2:** Shadow two live calls with your manager or a senior AE · Learn Vantage Flow's top five objections and the responses that work · Complete the product certification in Highspot
- **Week 3:** Co-facilitate a discovery call · Build your first target account list — 25 accounts that fit the ICP · Log every account in Salesforce before Friday
- **Week 4:** Run a discovery call solo (manager on mute, listening) · Present your pipeline in the team stand-up · Debrief with your manager and set your 60-day targets

**Slide 8 — Knowledge Check**
*Question:* A prospect responds to your outreach: "We're already using Monday.com and our team is used to it. I'm not sure switching makes sense." Which response best fits the Vantage sales approach?
- A) Send them a feature comparison document showing everything Vantage Flow does that Monday.com doesn't.
- B) Ask what "used to it" means in terms of outcomes — what Monday.com is and isn't delivering for their team today.
- C) Offer a free trial so they can compare the two products side by side.
- D) Acknowledge the objection and let them know you'll check back in next quarter.
*Correct Answer:* B
*Feedback — Correct:* Exactly right. "Used to it" is a surface objection — it tells you nothing about whether Monday.com is actually working for them. Asking about outcomes follows the MEDDIC principle of identifying pain before pitching. Once you know what they're struggling with, you have something real to respond to.
*Feedback — Incorrect:* Before you send a comparison doc, offer a trial, or walk away, you need to understand what the objection is actually about. "We use Monday.com" is not the same as "Monday.com is solving our problem." Ask what outcomes they're getting — that's where the real conversation starts.
