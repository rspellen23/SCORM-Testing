**Headline:** Your team has the knowledge. The tools are what's slowing you down.

**Body:**
Most L&D teams at mid-size companies are stuck between two bad options:

- **Enterprise authoring tools** (Articulate 360, Adobe Captivate) — powerful, but expensive, slow, and require a steep learning curve your team doesn't have time for.
- **DIY in PowerPoint or Canva** — fast to make, but not LMS-trackable, not branded consistently, and not scalable.

The result: courses that take weeks to build, look inconsistent, and don't track completion the way your LMS needs them to.

**Visual:** A split — left side shows a generic, off-brand PowerPoint deck; right side shows an L&D professional at a desk looking overwhelmed by timelines.

> **Speaker note:** This is where you pause and ask: "Does that sound familiar?" Mid-size L&D teams — usually 1–3 people — don't have the bandwidth that enterprise shops do. They need speed and polish, not a PhD in Articulate. Name the pain before you pitch the solution.

## Slide 4 — Introducing CourseCraft

**Headline:** Write it in plain language. Get a branded, LMS-ready course.

**Body:** CourseCraft turns a simple text script into a polished, interactive microlearning module — packaged as SCORM or cmi5, ready to upload to any LMS — in minutes.

No design software. No developers. No back-and-forth with an agency.

**How it works — three steps:**
1. **Write** — Draft your course content using our plain-language templates (no coding, no authoring tool)
2. **Build** — CourseCraft packages it into a branded, interactive HTML course with knowledge checks and completion tracking
3. **Upload** — Drop the zip file into your LMS. Done.

**Visual:** A clean three-step diagram: Script → CourseCraft → LMS-ready course.

> **Speaker note:** This is the "aha" slide. Keep it simple. The buyer doesn't need to understand how the engine works — they need to believe the three steps are real. If you have a live demo ready, this is a good moment to say "let me show you."

## Slide 11 — Appendix: FAQ

*Include this section at the back of the deck for buyers who want more detail. Don't present it — reference it if questions come up.*

**"What LMSes does CourseCraft work with?"**
Any LMS that supports SCORM 1.2, SCORM 2004, or cmi5/xAPI — including Intellum, Cornerstone, Docebo, Workday Learning, SAP SuccessFactors, and most others.

**"Do we need a developer to use it?"**
No. Courses are authored in plain text using our templates and built with a single command. No coding required.

**"Can we use our own brand kit?"**
Yes. CourseCraft applies your logo, colors, and fonts automatically to every course you build.

**"What if we need custom interactions — video, branching scenarios?"**
CourseCraft supports video, audio, and knowledge checks out of the box. Complex branching scenarios are on the roadmap. For highly custom interactions, we can discuss.

**"How long does it take to build a course?"**
Once your script is written, the build takes seconds. Writing the script using a template typically takes 2–4 hours for a 10–15 minute microlearning — versus days or weeks with traditional tools.
